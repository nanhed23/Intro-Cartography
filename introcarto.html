    Main Types of Maps

    This session, we will be looking at the basic types of maps. Maps, visual representations of an area that are used in Geography, consists of many different type. Here is a list of the most common ones.

  1. Climate Map
    2. A map that depicts the prevailing weather patterns of an area. It's made up of conventional map with overlaid colors to represent different climatatic zones and weather patterns that can based on the temperature, the amount of snow an area receives, an average number of cloudy days, etc.

  2. Economic/Resource Map
    3. A map that shows the many different world economic growth. It might be measured in GDP growth, PPP and per capita income growth. It is used to facilitate planning and forecasting the development and location of productive forces as well as for management of the national economy.

  3. Political Map
    4. A map that demonstrates governmental boundaries of countries, states, counties, and the location of major cities through the use of bright colors. It makes a political statement about which regions of the Earth belong to a country or state.

  4. Physical Map
    5. A map that indicates the location of natural landmarks/landforms like deserts, mountains, plains, large bodies of water, as well as major cities and country borders. It uses different shades of colors and lines to represent or show the many different terrain types of an area, such as the elevations and waters.

  5. Road Map
    6. A map that primarily displays roads and transport links rather than geographical information.

  6. Thematic Map
    7. A map that emphasizes on a particular theme or special topic and they are different from general reference maps because they do not just show natural features like rivers, cities, political subdivisions and highways .enhance one's understanding of the map's theme and purpose. This type of map is usually used in computer mapping or GIS. There are many types of thematic maps and lets name a few;
    • Choropleth Map
    • Dot Map
    • Graduated Circle Map
    • Isoline Map
    • Cartogram

  7. Topography Map
    8. A map that is characterized by large-scale detail and quantitative representation of relief that usually used contour lines (combination of two line segments that connect but do not intersect). It is detailed and has an accurate graphic representation of cultural and natural features on the ground.

GIS: Geographic Information System

In the modern world, the most essential tool of the cartographer has been the computers. Much of cartography, especially at the data-gathering survey level, has been subsumed by Geographic Information Systems (GIS).GIS is the merging of cartography, statistical analysis, and database technology. It allow users to create interactive queries, analyze spatial information, edit data in maps and much more through ArcGIS. GIS careers exist in every imaginable discipline, from environmental science to commercial businesses. More information about GIS can be found here.
